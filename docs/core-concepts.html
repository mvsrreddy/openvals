Core Concepts

Understand the underlying architecture and methodology of OpenVals.

Architecture

The Trust Score

The Trust Score is a single numerical value representing the model's reliability for a specific use case. It is calculated by taking a weighted average of individual metrics.

Trust Score = ∑ (weighti × metrici)

OpenVals evaluates models across several dimensions:

  • Accuracy (35%): How often the model matches the expected output.
  • Semantic Similarity (30%): Meaning-based comparison using vector embeddings.
  • Latency (15%): Response speed (lower is better).
  • Reliability (10%): Stability and structure of the output.
  • Safety (10%): Detection of unsafe or harmful content.

Dynamic Response Scoring (DRS)

DRS is a secondary scoring layer that evaluates the "naturalness" and "fluidity" of a model's response. Unlike standard metrics that look for a specific answer, DRS looks at the statistical properties of the text generation itself.

Recommendation Engine

The Recommendation Engine uses the results from multi-model benchmarks to suggest the best model for your specific needs. It doesn't just look at the highest score; it performs a tradeoff analysis:

  • Profiles: Choose from profiles like speed_optimized, accuracy_first, or balanced.
  • Risks: Identifies potential flags like "High Latency" or "Low Reliability".
  • Explanations: Provides a natural language reason why a specific model was chosen.

Benchmarking Flow

Benchmarking in OpenVals follows a multi-step process to ensure fair comparisons between models:

  1. Execution: Every model in the benchmark is run against the same dataset.
  2. Normalization: Scores are normalized using Min-Max scaling to ensure different metrics are comparable.
  3. Ranking: Models are ranked based on their total weighted normalized score.
💡 Pro Tip

Normalization is crucial because a latency of 2.0s might be "bad" in one context but "good" compared to other models in the same benchmark.

Extensibility

Everything in OpenVals is extensible. You can easily plug in:

  • Custom Models: By subclassing BaseModel.
  • Custom Metrics: By defining a function that takes output and expected.
  • Custom Normalization: By providing your own normalization logic.
